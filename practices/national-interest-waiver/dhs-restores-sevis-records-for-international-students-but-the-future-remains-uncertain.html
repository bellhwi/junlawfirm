DHS Restores SEVIS Records for International Students — But the Future Remains Uncertain

Three Key Strategies International Students Should Follow After SEVIS Record Restoration

Recently, the U.S. Department of Homeland Security (DHS) restored SEVIS (Student and Exchange Visitor Information System) records for many international students after abruptly deleting them without prior notice. Although this move has allowed many students to regain their valid status, DHS has made it clear that this action is temporary.

A DHS spokesperson explained that the agency has not reversed visa revocations themselves, but has temporarily reinstated SEVIS access for students whose visas were not revoked. Additionally, U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) stated during litigation that "a new policy for terminating SEVIS records is currently being developed, and until such guidance is issued, previously terminated records will either be restored or remain active." ICE emphasized that SEVIS records will no longer be terminated based solely on NCIC (National Crime Information Center) findings without additional review.

Despite the restoration, no new detailed guidance has been announced, and many international students continue to face uncertainty regarding their stay and academic pursuits in the United States. Colleges and universities are also concerned about the potential loss of international students and researchers, disruptions to research activities, and a weakening of the United States' competitiveness in higher education.

In this uncertain environment, it is essential for international students and universities to proactively prepare. Here are three key strategies:

Three Strategies International Students Should Follow

1. Regularly Monitor Your SEVIS Record Status

Since the DHS restoration is temporary, students must regularly check that their SEVIS record remains in “Active” status.
It is also important to maintain close communication with their Designated School Official (DSO) to detect and respond quickly to any irregularities or changes in their record.

Additionally, it is advisable to save or take screenshots of your SEVIS status periodically, as these records can serve as critical evidence if future issues arise.

2. Strictly Comply with Visa Maintenance Requirements

Given the possibility of future policy changes, it is crucial to strictly adhere to all visa maintenance rules.
This includes maintaining full-time enrollment and ensuring that any employment activities, such as CPT or OPT, are properly authorized before beginning work.
Unauthorized employment can have serious consequences for maintaining status.

For those planning to apply for OPT, STEM OPT extensions, or other programs, understanding the eligibility requirements and application timelines is critical.

3. Review and Strategically Prepare Your Post-Graduation Plans

To prepare for any future uncertainties or regulatory changes, students should proactively map out their post-graduation options, such as applying for OPT, pursuing a STEM OPT extension, or seeking H-1B sponsorship.
It is advisable to consult with your DSO or an experienced immigration attorney to carefully review your immigration plan and adjust timelines as needed.

Being proactive about future applications and extensions can prevent last-minute issues.

Conclusion

While DHS’s restoration of SEVIS records offers temporary relief, the future remains uncertain.
International students must stay vigilant by monitoring their SEVIS status, strictly complying with visa requirements, and strategically preparing their immigration plans.

Our office will continue to monitor developments and provide timely updates as DHS issues new policies.
Remaining proactive and well-prepared is the best strategy to navigate these ongoing challenges.
